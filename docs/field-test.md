# Field Test Runbook

Use this runbook before a real Wasl beta pilot. Keep the test lightweight and
close to the restaurant's real operating conditions.

## 30-minute pre-beta test plan

1. Open the public menu on iPhone Safari and Android Chrome.
2. Scan one printed general QR and one printed table QR.
3. Browse categories, add items, remove items, and submit one pickup order.
4. Submit one table order from a table QR.
5. If delivery is enabled, submit one delivery order with phone and address.
6. Refresh the public page during a pending order and confirm the retry/check
   state is understandable.
7. Open dashboard orders on mobile and laptop.
8. Move the order through pending, preparing, ready, and completed.
9. Open and print/preview the kitchen ticket.
10. Download one order export.

## 2-hour first-service pilot plan

1. Run during a low-volume service window.
2. Keep one staff device open on dashboard orders.
3. Keep a manual ordering fallback ready.
4. Use printed QR codes in their real table positions.
5. Ask staff to refresh orders regularly.
6. Let a small number of real guests order through Wasl.
7. Print or preview tickets for each order.
8. Record slow network, customer confusion, and staff friction immediately.
9. Export orders after service.
10. Review feedback with the owner before widening usage.

## Before service

- Confirm QR links still work after menu and branding edits.
- Confirm delivery is described as restaurant-owned delivery.
- Confirm payments, WhatsApp Business, and POS are described as coming soon or
  foundation, not live integrations.
- Confirm images are optional and compressed.
- Confirm menu works without photos.
- Confirm owner login and staff access expectations.

## After service

- Count successful orders and failed/retried attempts.
- Review ticket readability with kitchen staff.
- Check whether any guest needed help scanning QR codes.
- Check whether staff could see delivery address and phone quickly.
- Review exports and statistics.
- List blockers before the next service.

## Staff questions

- Was the menu easy for customers to open?
- Did new orders appear clearly?
- Were status buttons easy to use on mobile?
- Was ticket printing readable?
- Were delivery and pickup options clear?
- What slowed the team down?
- What should be changed before the next pilot?

## Feedback to record

- Device and browser used.
- Network condition.
- QR position and lighting.
- Order type: table, pickup, or delivery.
- Any duplicate tap, pending, retry, or refresh behavior.
- Staff comments.
- Customer comments.

## Rollback plan

If the restaurant struggles during service:

1. Stop inviting new guests to scan QR codes.
2. Keep existing dashboard orders open until handled.
3. Switch to the restaurant's normal manual ordering flow.
4. Export orders if useful for reconciliation.
5. Record the blocker and fix it before the next test.
